How I Made $500/Month with a Niche Website

A transparent breakdown of how a simple AI tutorial website generates passive income through Google AdSense and affiliate marketing.

By Qi Scan Team · Jun 16, 2026 · 6 min read

The Numbers

Let me be completely transparent. Here's exactly how my AI tutorial website makes $500/month:

  • Google AdSense: $280/month (56%)
  • Affiliate commissions: $180/month (36%)
  • Sponsorship/featured spots: $40/month (8%)
  • Total: $500/month

The Traffic Breakdown

This revenue comes from approximately 15,000 monthly page views, distributed across:

  • Organic search: 70% (10,500 views)
  • Social media (Pinterest, Twitter): 20% (3,000 views)
  • Direct/bookmarks: 10% (1,500 views)

How I Built It

The site covers AI tools for web development — a niche I chose because:

  1. High search demand (people actively looking for solutions)
  2. Good affiliate programs (many AI tools offer 20-30% recurring commissions)
  3. I could create genuinely useful content (personal experience)

I built the site using AI-assisted development (see our tutorial on building websites with AI), published 40+ articles over 6 months, and focused heavily on SEO from day one.

Key Lessons Learned

  • Consistency beats perfection: Publishing regularly is more important than writing flawless articles
  • Long-tail keywords are gold: "best AI tool for responsive web design in 2026" has less competition and converts better than just "web design tools"
  • Internal linking matters: Linking related articles together keeps visitors on your site longer
  • Update old content: Refreshing articles quarterly keeps them ranking

Is This Scalable?

Absolutely. Many site owners run portfolios of 5-10 niche sites, each generating $300-1000/month. The marginal cost of each additional site decreases as you refine your process.

The key is treating it like a real business, not a get-rich-quick scheme. It took me 6 months to reach my first dollar and 12 months to hit consistent $500/month. But the traffic compounds over time — today's articles are still bringing in visitors a year later.
