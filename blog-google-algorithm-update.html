Google's Latest Algorithm Update: What You Need to Know

Understanding the June 2026 Core Update and how it affects AI-generated content. Practical tips to protect your rankings.

By Qi Scan Team · Jun 14, 2026 · 5 min read

The June 2026 Core Update

Google rolled out its latest core algorithm update in early June 2026, and the webmaster community is buzzing. Here's what changed, why it matters, and what you should do about it.

What Changed?

The core update focuses on three main areas:

  1. Content quality signals: Enhanced detection of low-effort, AI-generated content that lacks original insight or expertise
  2. User experience metrics: Stronger weighting of Core Web Vitals and interaction metrics
  3. E-E-A-T reinforcement: Experience and authoritativeness signals are now weighted more heavily than ever

Impact on AI-Generated Content

The biggest concern: is Google penalizing AI content? The answer is nuanced. Google isn't banning AI-generated content �?it's demoting low-quality AI content. Content that provides genuine value, even if assisted by AI, continues to rank well.

Key takeaway: Google evaluates the quality of the final content, not how it was created. If your article helps people, it will rank �?regardless of AI involvement.

What Should You Do?

1. Audit Your Top Pages

Check Google Search Console for significant traffic changes. Pages that lost traffic likely had thin content or poor user experience signals.

2. Improve E-E-A-T Signals

  • Add author bios with credentials
  • Cite reputable sources
  • Show real-world experience and examples
  • Build genuine backlinks through outreach

3. Optimize Technical SEO

Looking Forward

Algorithm updates will continue to favor quality and user satisfaction. The websites that thrive are those that genuinely serve their audience �?AI is just a tool to help you do that better.
